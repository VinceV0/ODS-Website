Partners

We've made a strategic decision to focus on our core expertise in satellite technology rather than developing our own launch vehicles. That's why a collaboration with an experienced launch provider is so crucial: with the aid of a joint venture, we want to put our satellite into orbit to send it on missions investigating the other planets in our local solar system. To achieve these ambitious mission goals, we're actively seeking partnerships for our satellite deployment.

Rocket Lab

Rocket Lab, a New Zealand-based company with a proven track record of satellite launches, Rocket Lab offers a range of launch solutions and services primarily focused on the small to medium satellite market, with a key focus on precision and responsiveness. Rocket Lab also has strong ties to the United States, with its head office located there. They host a global network of radio telescope hardware and digital networks essential for sending and receiving radio frequency data from orbital and deep space missions.

Yusen Logistics

Our project requires a highly specialized and secure logistics solution for the transportation of our satellite to the Rocket Lab launch site. Given the satellite's sensitive nature and high value, ensuring its safe and reliable delivery is paramount to the mission's success. To meet these critical requirements, we will partner with Yusen Logistics.

New Zealand Government

Operating orbital or deep space machinery requires governmental approval, to ensure safety, security, and compliance with international obligations. This comprehensive approval process is primarily overseen by the New Zealand Space Agency (NZSA), which operates under the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE).
