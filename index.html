ABSTRACT

This joint project proposal details the development and mission of a robotic satellite designed for interplanetary exploration. The satellite, developed and fabricated by ODS, is distinguished by its propellant-less propulsion system and a suite of advanced scientific instruments. The mission's objectives include demonstrating a long-duration, multi-target exploration capability and collecting unprecedented data on celestial bodies. The satellite's launch will be managed by our strategic partner, Rocket Lab, following rigorous pre-launch testing and government authorization. Successful mission execution will validate a new era of cost-effective, long-term space missions and provide invaluable scientific data to the global community.

Introduction

The next frontier of space exploration is not just about reaching new destinations, but about staying there for extended, multi-target missions. This project, a groundbreaking venture into interplanetary exploration, aims to redefine mission longevity and scientific return.

We're developing a robotic satellite designed to venture beyond Earth's gravitational confines for years, fueled by a revolutionary propellant-less propulsion system. Our objectives are to gather unprecedented data from celestial bodies and advance our understanding of the solar system's origins, all while proving the viability of this next-generation technology.

Traditional deep-space missions are severely limited by the need for chemical fuel, restricting their duration and the number of celestial bodies they can explore. Our project provides a game-changing solution: a robotic satellite that uses an innovative, propellant-less propulsion system. By continuously generating thrust without consuming fuel, our satellite can achieve missions of unprecedented length and flexibility, opening up new possibilities for exploring the outer solar system.

The impact of this project will be profound, ushering in a new era of space exploration. It will not only deliver unprecedented scientific data on planetary composition, gravitational fields, and potentially metallic asteroid resources, but also establish a proven, cost-effective model for long-duration space missions. This venture will significantly advance humanity's understanding of the cosmos, open new avenues for resource utilization, and solidify our position as a leader in innovative space technology.
